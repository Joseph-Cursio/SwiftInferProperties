## 1. Why this cycle is different

PRD §17.3's empirical-tuning loop has produced three measurement points (cycles 6 + 14 + 17 = 26.7% → 34.8% → 52.3%). v1.21 + v1.22 ship the largest mechanism-cycle-pair surface delta in the loop's history (-183 candidates across cycles 18 + 19) — a **precision-positive movement** measured by the per-mechanism analysis but not yet validated against an empirical rate measurement. Cycle 20 closes that gap.

The cycle-19 findings doc projected:
> Provisional aggregate projection: **57-65%** (continued upward from cycle-17's 52.3%, reflecting cycle-18+19's precision-positive movement removing 22 reject picks across the cycle-18-→-cycle-20 surface).

The actual cycle-20 number tests two hypotheses:

1. **The precision-positive cycle-18+19 movement is recall-neutral.** If the closure of -183 candidates was 100% reject-class (no accepts removed), the aggregate moves **57-65%** as projected. Outcome A.
2. **The closure swept some accepts.** If V1.22.B's both-sides direction-counter or V1.22.D's stride-style veto closed cycle-17 accepts (e.g., the Algo `endOfChunk × startOfChunk` was cycle-14/17 ACCEPT — V1.22.D demoted it per the calibration-trade-off rationale), the aggregate moves less than projected. Outcome B (50-57%).

The cycle-20 measurement also produces the **first per-template rate for two cycle-19 carry-forward classes**:
- **Class 14 (V1.22.C fixed-point-name positive signal):** zero surfacing on the cycle-1..14 corpora, so cycle-20 sample on these corpora can't measure its per-template rate. Cycle-20 acknowledges class 14's recall-positive infrastructure is in place but unmeasurable on the current corpora.
- **Asymmetric cross-pair noise (V1.22.B variance):** cycle-20 sample triages the surviving asymmetric pairs (`index(after:) × _minimumCapacity(forScale:)` etc.) to validate cycle-19 priority #2's projected magnitude.

Cycle 20 measures whether the **+25.6pp aggregate movement from cycle-6 to cycle-17** continues to **+30-35pp from cycle-6 to cycle-20** (i.e., whether the cycle-18+19 mechanism cycles produced sustained acceleration or whether the +17.5pp cycle-14→cycle-17 delta was a one-time large step from new-template introduction that slows in subsequent cycles).

## 8. v1.23 trajectory framing

PRD v1.0 §17.3 names calibration as "the operationalization of high precision, low recall."

- **Cycle 6 (v1.9):** 26.7% on 349-surface (baseline).
- **Cycle 14 (v1.17):** 34.8% on 229-surface (+8.1pp / 8 mechanism cycles).
- **Cycle 17 (v1.20):** 52.3% on 335-surface (+17.5pp / 3 mechanism cycles + new-class introduction).
- **Cycle 20 (v1.23, this release):** ?% on 152-surface — **the loop's first four-point trajectory**.

The measurement closes a meta-question that's been open since cycle 17:

> **Did the v1.21 + v1.22 precision-positive cycle continue the cycle-17 acceleration, plateau, or regress? Per-mechanism: which of the cycle-18+19 workstreams had the largest per-construction precision impact?**

Cycle 20's headline number answers this directly. Possible outcomes:

- **Outcome A: Aggregate ≥ 60%.** v1.21 + v1.22 mechanism work shifted the rate further in the cycle-17 direction. The +25.6pp cycle-6→cycle-17 shift extends to +33-43pp. §19 ≥70% target is +5-10pp from cycle 20; one or two more mechanism cycles reach it.
- **Outcome B: Aggregate 53-60%.** Modest improvement from cycle 17. The +25.6pp cycle-6→cycle-17 shift extends to +26-33pp. §19 target is +10-17pp; two or three more mechanism cycles reach it.
- **Outcome C: Aggregate ≈ 52% (flat vs cycle 17).** v1.21 + v1.22 contributed precision-only — the closure was 100% reject-class but the surviving pool's per-template rates didn't improve. The +17.5pp cycle-14→cycle-17 step was a one-time delta from new-template introduction (dual-style + lifted classes). §19 target is +18pp; ≥3 more mechanism cycles needed.
- **Outcome D: Aggregate < 52%.** Counter-intuitive; v1.21 + v1.22 swept some accepts. Most likely culprit: V1.22.D's stride-style veto closing the cycle-14/17-ACCEPT Algo `endOfChunk × startOfChunk` triple (calibration trade-off). Methodology investigation: validate the cycle-20 verdict on the surviving 18 round-trip + 21 idempotence-lifted picks.

The cycle-21 priority list rotates accordingly:

- **Outcome A or B**: continue mechanism cycles (asymmetric label counter + FP approximate-equality + math-library `_relaxed*` extension).
- **Outcome C**: explicit pivot to PRD §20 v1.1+ items (SemanticIndex first); the empirical loop has saturated on the four cycle-1..14 corpora.
- **Outcome D**: revert-cycle to validate the V1.22.D demotion; may downgrade `StrideStyleLabels.curated` if accepts are being suppressed.

After v1.23 ships:
- The loop has measured aggregate rates **four times** (cycles 6 + 14 + 17 + 20). Cadence: every 2-3 mechanism cycles → 1 empirical cycle (post-cycle-14: 8 mechanism cycles before re-measurement; post-cycle-17: 2 mechanism cycles before re-measurement — accelerating cadence).
- The next empirical cycle would naturally land at cycle 22-23 (after 2-3 mechanism cycles), unless cycle-20 outcome demands earlier.

The natural cadence stays at one mechanism per release. After cycle 20's measurement data, the §19 acceptance-rate target should be projectable from four data points: if the trajectory is clearly headed toward 70%, continue mechanism cycles; if it's plateaued, pivot. v1.23 is the data point that lets that judgment land empirically.

No active milestone plan opens with the v1.23 commit. Subsequent work picks one of the §20 items, the post-v1.23 priority list, or a fresh refactor target.
