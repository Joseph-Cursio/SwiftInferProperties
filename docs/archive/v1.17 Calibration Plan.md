Working doc for the SwiftInferProperties v1.17 cut. v1.17 is the **fourteenth calibration cycle** — closes post-v1.16 priority #2 (Possible-tier re-sampling on the post-v1.16 229-surface) and runs the §17.3 loop a fourteenth time. **Second empirical-only cycle** (after cycle 6 = v1.9). Targets the long-overdue per-template acceptance-rate measurement that has been carried forward across six consecutive mechanism cycles (cycles 7-13).

> **v1.17 is a cycle-14 measurement release** — zero Sources/ changes, zero test changes, zero behavior changes; v1.17 binary-equivalent to v1.16.0 except the version-string bump. The cycle's deliverable is **per-template + per-corpus acceptance-rate data on the post-v1.16 229-surface**, comparable point-for-point to cycle 6's measurement on the post-V1.8.1 349-surface (the previous baseline).

**Ephemeral** — archive to `docs/archive/v1.17 Calibration Plan.md` once the tag is pushed and the §13 + §16 release-blocking criteria still hold.

## Why this cycle is different

PRD §17.3's empirical-tuning loop has two steps: **suppress noise** + **measure acceptance**. Cycles 1-5 + 7-13 ran the suppression step; cycle 6 ran the measurement step on the 349-surface (acceptance rate **26.7% = 12/45**, with 5 unknown). Six mechanism cycles (cycles 7-13) have shipped between cycle 6 and now — total noise-floor reduction 349 → 229 (**−34.4%** over six cycles) — and the question the loop must answer is: **has the per-template acceptance rate moved measurably**, or is the suppression work just shifting the noise around?

The cycle-6 baseline rates were:
- monotonicity 4/5 = 80%
- round-trip 6/14 = 43%
- associativity 2/5 = 40%
- commutativity 1/5 = 20%
- idempotence 0/10 = 0%
- inverse-pair 0/5 = 0%
- identity-element 0/1 = 0%
- **Aggregate: 12/45 = 26.7%** (5 unknown excluded)

After cycles 7-13:
- idempotence + inverse-pair + round-trip have all gained at least one mechanism that suppresses the cycle-6 rejected pool (cycles 7-9 + 11 + 12 + 13).
- The cycle-6 0/5 inverse-pair rate is now **stale** (rejected pool empty post-v1.14).
- The cycle-6 0/10 idempotence rate is now stale (cycle-7 + cycle-12 + cycle-13 suppressed all 10 rejection picks).
- The cycle-6 6/14 round-trip rate is partially stale (cycle-9 + cycle-12 + cycle-13 suppressed multiple rejection picks, but the pool also has new shape compositions).

**Cycle-14 measures whether the per-template acceptance rate has crossed the PRD §19 ≥70% target on any template** and whether the aggregate has moved from 26.7% toward that target. The measurement is value-creating regardless of outcome — a flat result tells us suppression is exhausted on the existing corpora; an upward-shift result confirms the loop is working; a counter-intuitive movement (e.g., round-trip *down* despite three direction-counter cycles + one SetAlgebra-shape cycle on it) flags a methodology gap.

## Open decisions

1. **Sample size: 50 (matches cycle 6) vs larger.** Default proposal: **(a) 50.** Rationale: direct comparability with cycle 6's per-template rates; same single-session rater fatigue level; the loop's stratification methodology is calibrated for 50. Larger sample (75 / 100) would buy statistical power at the cost of session fatigue + delayed v1.17 ship.

2. **Single-runner Claude vs multi-rater.** Default proposal: **(a) single-runner Claude** (matches cycle 6). The cycle-6 plan's open-decision-#2 framing holds: a second human rater would let v1.17 ship inter-rater agreement rates, but no second rater is available. Single-runner caveat documented in the rubric (carried forward from cycle 6 verbatim).

3. **Stratification: cycle-6-matching weights vs proportional-to-surface.** Default proposal: **(a) cycle-6-matching weights** (per the table above). Rationale: enables direct per-template rate-comparison; the four-pick reallocation from inverse-pair → round-trip is the only deviation, and it's forced by the v1.16 surface (only 1 inverse-pair candidate). Proportional weights would over-weight ComplexModule's 136 round-trip surface to ~30 picks (out of 50) and starve every other template — losing per-template measurement.

4. **Cycle-6 picks reuse vs fresh sampling.** Default proposal: **(a) fresh stratified sampling** (matches cycle 6's posture). Rationale: preserves independent-measurement integrity; the cycle-6 picks are now mostly suppressed (cycle-13 closed the last 6 SetAlgebra-shape picks; only the lone Algo stride-style inverse-pair pick survives). Reusing the surviving cycle-6 picks where possible would bias toward the worst-case false-positive class. Fresh sampling produces a representative v1.16 view.

5. **Refresh rubric vs carry-forward verbatim.** Default proposal: **(b) carry-forward verbatim with cycle-14 supplement.** New `docs/cycle-14-triage-rubric.md` carries cycle-6's per-template criteria verbatim and adds a "Post-cycle-6 mechanism context" section documenting the suppression layers a candidate has passed (cycles 7-13) — useful for the rater to know that a surviving candidate has cleared multiple prior gates. The cycle-6 rubric stays unchanged.

6. **Tier mix: Possible-only (default) vs include some Likely-tier.** Default proposal: **(a) include the lone Likely-tier identity-element pick** (the `rescaledDivide(_:_:)` × `Complex.zero` survivor). Same as cycle 6's posture: the Likely-tier outlier is included because it's the only identity-element survivor; the rest of the sample is Possible-tier. Rationale: maximize cross-template coverage at fixed sample size.

7. **Cycle numbering: cycle 14 = v1.17.** Contiguous with v1.16 = cycle 13, v1.15 = cycle 12, v1.14 = cycle 11; v1.13 = cycle 10 (refactor). **Second empirical-only cycle** in the loop's history (after v1.9 = cycle 6).

8. **Whether to commit per-decision evidence files.** Default proposal: **yes** — the `triage-notes.md` per-decision rationale lets a future re-run validate Claude's verdicts; the `triage-decisions.json` machine-readable schema enables programmatic comparison with cycle 6.

## v1.17 trajectory framing

PRD v1.0 §17.3 names calibration as "the operationalization of high precision, low recall."

Cycle 6 (v1.9) introduced empirical measurement on the 349-surface — **26.7% aggregate acceptance** (12/45, 5 unknown).
Cycles 7-13 (v1.10-v1.16) shipped six mechanism cycles + one refactor cycle, suppressing the surface from 349 → 229 (**−34.4%**).
Cycle 14 (v1.17, this release) re-measures on the 229-surface — the loop's first **same-methodology re-measurement** since cycle 6.

The measurement closes a meta-question that's been open since cycle 6:

> **Does cumulative noise-floor suppression (-34.4% across 6 mechanism cycles) translate to measurably-higher per-template acceptance rates, or has it just been shifting the noise around?**

Cycle 14's headline number answers this directly. Possible outcomes (for narrative-doc planning):
- **Outcome A: Aggregate ≥ 50%.** Suppression is paying off; the loop is working as designed. Continue mechanism cycles toward the §19 ≥70% target.
- **Outcome B: Aggregate 30-50%.** Modest improvement; mechanism cycles are precision-positive but recall is also dropping (i.e., the cycle-6 acceptance pool has shrunk along with the rejection pool). Document in findings.
- **Outcome C: Aggregate ≈ 26.7% (flat).** Suppression has been precision-only; the cycle-6 numerator (12 accepted) and denominator (45 known) have moved together. Saturation signal — pivot to v1.1+ trajectory (SemanticIndex etc.).
- **Outcome D: Aggregate < 26.7%.** Counter-intuitive; suppression has removed acceptable candidates faster than rejected ones. Methodology problem — investigate cycle-by-cycle which mechanism overshot.

Whichever outcome lands, the cycle-15 priority list rotates accordingly:
- Outcome A or B: continue with stride-style label extension (priority #1 from cycle 13's list, now demoted to v1.18) + reference-type carrier counter etc.
- Outcome C: explicit pivot to PRD §20 v1.1+ items (SemanticIndex first).
- Outcome D: emergency revert-cycle to identify which mechanism overshot.

After v1.17 ships:
- The loop has measured aggregate rates twice (cycle 6 + cycle 14) and shipped 6 mechanism cycles between them.
- The next empirical cycle would naturally land at cycle 20+ (every 6 mechanism cycles), unless cycle-14 outcome demands an earlier re-measurement.

The natural cadence stays at one mechanism per release. After cycle 15 + the v1.17 measurement data, the §19 acceptance-rate target should be projectable: if the trajectory is clearly headed toward 70%, continue mechanism cycles; if it's plateaued, pivot. v1.17 is the data point that lets that judgment land empirically.

No active milestone plan opens with the v1.17 commit. Subsequent work picks one of the §20 items, the post-v1.17 priority list, or a fresh refactor target and opens a fresh `docs/<plan>.md`.
