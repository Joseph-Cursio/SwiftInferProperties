# v1.11 Calibration Plan

Working doc for the SwiftInferProperties v1.11 cut. v1.11 is the **eighth calibration cycle** — closes cycle-8 priority #1 from `docs/calibration-cycle-7-findings.md` (inverse-pair template direction-label counter-signal) and runs the §17.3 loop an eighth time. **Second data-driven cycle**: v1.11 ports v1.10's just-shipped, just-verified mechanism (`Signal.Kind.directionLabel` on `IdempotenceTemplate`) onto the next-most-rejected template (`InversePairTemplate`, cycle-6 measured at 0/5 = 0% Possible-tier acceptance).

> **v1.11 is the second cycle whose mechanism is empirically motivated, and the first to *replicate* a verified cycle's mechanism on an adjacent template.** Cycle-7 closed the hypothesis → mechanism → measurement → tuning loop on idempotence. Cycle-8 takes the same closed loop and applies it to inverse-pair, where cycle-6's 0/5 acceptance rate ranked it tied-second (with identity-element 0/1, which has too small a sample to drive a release).

**Ephemeral** — archive to `docs/archive/v1.11 Calibration Plan.md` once the tag is pushed and the §13 + §16 release-blocking criteria still hold.

Two sub-patterns inside the direction-labeled candidates:

Cycle-9 candidate.

## What v1.11 ships

Two deliverables (mirroring the v1.10 shape):

1. **`InversePairTemplate` direction-label counter-signal.** Re-uses the existing `Signal.Kind.directionLabel` enum case shipped in v1.10. New private helper `InversePairTemplate.directionLabelCounterSignal(for:)` returning weight `-10` (note the **−10**, not −15 — see open decision #1) when *either* side of the pair's first-parameter argument label matches the curated direction set:
   ```swift
   // Reuses IdempotenceTemplate.directionLabels — see open decision #2.
   ```
   Score arithmetic for inverse-pair (baseline `+25` typeSymmetry vs idempotence's `+30`):
   - typeSymmetry alone: `+25` → Possible tier (20-39 range).
   - typeSymmetry + direction counter: `+25 − 10 = +15` → Suppressed tier (< 20).
   - typeSymmetry + curated/project name match (`+10`) + direction counter: `+25 + 10 − 10 = +25` → Possible tier (still surfaced, clean margin).
   
   Curated inverse-pair name matches (e.g., `encode/decode`, `parse/format`) keep surfacing even when direction labels coincidentally appear; bare-shape directional pairs drop below the Possible threshold.

2. **Cycle-8 calibration run + findings writeup.** Re-run `swift-infer discover --include-possible` against the four cycle-1+2+3+4+5+6+7 corpora at the v1.11 commit; capture post-counter-signal snapshots to `docs/calibration-cycle-8-data/`. Compare suppression deltas vs the cycle-7 post-direction-counter baseline (the 296-surface). Findings doc at `docs/calibration-cycle-8-findings.md` documents per-corpus delta + verification (re-test cycle-6 inverse-pair picks #45-#49 against the v1.11 binary; expect 2 of 5 to suppress, 3 of 5 to stay surfaced) + cycle-9 hypotheses.

PRD §19 sets the long-term acceptance-rate target at ≥ 70% after 6 months of dogfooding; v1.11 is cycle 8 of that loop and the second to *target* a measured rate-improvement.

## Open decisions

1. **Counter-signal weight: -10 vs -15.** Two choices: (a) **-10 (this plan's default)**; (b) -15 (mirrors v1.10 verbatim). Default proposal: **(a) -10.** Rationale: inverse-pair's typeSymmetry baseline is `+25` (vs idempotence's `+30`), so the math differs:
   - **-10**: bare → `+15` Suppressed (clean margin from 20 boundary); curated/project name `+10` → `+25` Possible (clean margin).
   - **-15**: bare → `+10` Suppressed (cleaner margin); curated/project name `+10` → `+20` Possible (sits *exactly on* the tier boundary — the noisy zone v1.10's open decision #1 explicitly avoided).
   
   The -10 weight preserves curated/project-vocabulary inverse-pair name matches (e.g., `encode/decode`, `parse/format`, `serialize/deserialize`) cleanly in Possible tier even when a direction label coincidentally appears. The -15 weight is more aggressive but risks suppressing legitimate inverse-pair candidates that happen to use a stride-style argument label. Conservative-precision posture (PRD §3.5) prefers the cleaner-margin option.

2. **Where the curated `directionLabels` set lives.** Three choices: (a) **reuse `IdempotenceTemplate.directionLabels` directly (this plan)**; (b) hoist to a new `SwiftInferCore.DirectionLabels` namespace; (c) duplicate as `InversePairTemplate.directionLabels`. Default proposal: **(a) reuse.** Rationale: v1.10 already declared `directionLabels` as `public static`; cross-template reuse is the cleanest factoring for the second consumer. Hoisting (option b) is premature — wait until cycle-9's `RoundTripTemplate` becomes the third consumer, then refactor to a shared namespace as a v1.13 atomic move. Duplicating (option c) creates drift risk: if cycle-10 extends the set, two locations need updating. Reuse is one-call-site, single-source-of-truth, easy to refactor later.

3. **Either-side vs forward-only detection.** Two choices: (a) **either-side (this plan)** — fires when forward's *or* reverse's first-param label is in the curated set; (b) forward-only — fires only on forward's first-param label. Default proposal: **(a) either-side.** Rationale: the dominant Algo pattern is `index(after:) × index(before:)` (forward `after`, reverse `before`) — *both* sides are direction-labeled, but a forward-only check would still fire because forward has `after`. The interesting edge case is *asymmetric* labeling: `format(_:) × parse(after:)` — forward has no label, reverse has `after`. Forward-only would miss this; either-side catches it. False-positive risk: a legitimate curated-name pair with one direction-labeled side (e.g., `format(after:) × parse(_:)`) — but the curated/project name match's `+10` keeps it in Possible tier with -10 counter (`+25 + 10 − 10 = +25`). Either-side detection is strictly more sensitive without false-positive cost given the -10 weight.

4. **Whether to commit cycle-8 decisions data.** Default: **yes** — `docs/calibration-cycle-8-data/` mirroring cycles 1-7. Files small.

5. **Whether to bundle cycle-8 priorities #1 + #2 (round-trip direction counter) into v1.11.** Two choices: (a) **ship priority #1 only (this plan)**; (b) bundle 1+2. Default proposal: **(a) priority #1 only.** Rationale: same "one rule per release" precedent as v1.5-1.10. Bundling muddies per-template attribution. Round-trip's surface is much larger than inverse-pair's, so bundling would also bloat the calibration measurement burden.

6. **Re-run cadence.** Default: **post-counter-signal only** — cycle-7's post-direction-counter snapshots at `docs/calibration-cycle-7-data/` are the cycle-8 pre-counter-signal baseline. No need to re-run cycle-7's snapshots.

7. **Pre-existing inverse-pair signals' interaction with the direction counter.** The template currently emits five signals: `typeSymmetrySignature` (+25), `exactNameMatch` (+10, curated/project), `floatingPointStorage` (-10, FP-only counter), `nonDeterministicBody` (veto), `protocolCoverageVeto` (veto). The new direction counter is the second non-veto negative signal alongside `floatingPointStorage`. Score interaction matrix:
   ```
                         Direction:            no       yes (-10)
   FP-storage: no    Bare:                 +25 P    +15 SUPP
                     Curated name (+10):   +35 P    +25 P
   FP-storage: yes (-10)  Bare:            +15 SUPP +5  SUPP
                          Curated name:    +25 P    +15 SUPP
   ```
   Both counters compose correctly: any single counter against bare typeSymmetry → Suppressed; both counters against curated name → Suppressed (legitimate behavior — FP storage *and* direction labels means the pair is unlikely to be a true inverse). Default: this is the desired behavior, no veto-collapse needed.

8. **Tier-boundary collision risk.** Cycle-6 inverse-pair picks all surfaced at Score 25 (Possible). The counter -10 against +25 baseline: 15 → Suppressed (clean). Against +35 (curated): 25 → Possible (clean). Default: this matches the design intent — direction-labeled bare-shape pairs suppress; curated/project name matches preserve.

## v1.11 trajectory framing

PRD v1.0 §17.3 names calibration as "the operationalization of high precision, low recall — without it, the philosophy is just a slogan." Cycle 7 (v1.10) was the first cycle to ship a structural change *targeted at a measured signal*. Cycle 8 (v1.11) is the first to *replicate a verified mechanism on an adjacent template* — confirming v1.10's `Signal.Kind.directionLabel` enum case is the right factoring (the same case serves two consumers), confirming the curated 10-element direction-label set is portable across templates with different baseline scores, and confirming cross-template reuse via `public static` works without a refactor pass.

After v1.11 ships:
- The cycle-6 0/5 inverse-pair acceptance rate has a measurement-anchored fix in-tree.
- Cycle-9 can re-measure (sample anew, or re-triage cycle-6's inverse-pair picks at the v1.11 binary) and confirm whether the rate moves up from 0%.
- The data-driven priority list scales: cycles 9 + 10 + 11 will pick up priorities #2 (round-trip direction counter) + #3 (FP approximate-equality) + the domain-mismatch family (idempotence + inverse-pair scale-vs-capacity) from cycle-7's findings.

The natural cadence stays at one cycle per release. After cycle 11 or so, the §19 acceptance-rate target should be measurable across multiple per-template improvements, and the calibration loop's saturation should become visible (when re-sampling the cycle-1 picks at v1.13 produces ≥ 70% acceptance, the loop has reached its target).

After the calibration loop reaches steady state, the natural next contributions return to the §20 v1.1+ trajectory.

No active milestone plan opens with the v1.11 commit. Subsequent work picks one of the §20 items or the cycle-9+ priority list and opens a fresh `docs/<plan>.md`.
