# v1.20 Calibration Plan

Working doc for the SwiftInferProperties v1.20 cut. v1.20 is the **seventeenth calibration cycle** and the **third empirical-only cycle** in the loop's history (after cycle 6 = v1.9 and cycle 14 = v1.17). Targets the per-template + per-corpus acceptance-rate measurement on the cumulative **post-v1.18 + post-v1.19 surface** — the first re-measurement since cycle 14, with three mechanism cycles between (cycle 15 = v1.18 Workstreams A + C, cycle 16 = v1.19 Workstream B).

> **v1.20 is a cycle-17 measurement release** — zero `Sources/` changes, zero test changes, zero behavior changes; v1.20 binary-equivalent to v1.19.0 except the version-string bump. The cycle's deliverable is **per-template + per-corpus acceptance-rate data on the post-v1.19 surface**, comparable point-for-point to cycle 6's measurement on the post-V1.8.1 349-surface (26.7%) and cycle 14's measurement on the post-V1.16.1 229-surface (34.8%).

**Ephemeral** — archive to `docs/archive/v1.20 Calibration Plan.md` once the tag is pushed and the §13 + §16 release-blocking criteria still hold.

## Why this cycle is different

PRD §17.3's empirical-tuning loop has two steps: **suppress noise** + **measure acceptance**. Cycles 1–5 + 7–13 + 15 + 16 ran the suppression step (12 mechanism cycles + 1 refactor cycle); cycles 6 + 14 ran the measurement step. v1.18 (cycle 15) and v1.19 (cycle 16) ship the largest mechanism deltas the loop has seen since M8.5 — three new mechanism classes (carrier-kind structural counter, dual-style pair detection + consistency template, lift admission via value-semantic gate + composition-template additive-monoid scoring) that broaden the addressable function surface from `non-mutating only` to `non-mutating ∪ value-semantic-mutating`.

The per-cycle measurement history:

| Cycle | Surface | Aggregate acceptance rate | Δ vs prior |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| 6 | 349 | 26.7% (12/45, 5 unknown) | (baseline) |
| 14 | 229 | 34.8% (16/46, 4 unknown) | +8.1pp |
| 17 | TBD (capture in V1.20.A) | TBD | TBD |

Three mechanism cycles ship between cycles 14 and 17 (v1.17 itself was empirical-only):

- **v1.18 Workstream A** — Carrier kind signal (+5 / -10) on Idempotence + RoundTrip + InversePair + IdentityElement. Expected effect: precision-positive on value-semantic carriers (small accept boost), precision-positive on reference-type carriers (suppression).
- **v1.18 Workstream C** — Dual-style consistency template. Expected effect: recall-positive (introduces a new accept class on canonical Swift dual-style siblings); high-precision by construction (75 → Strong; pairing constraint requires both members on the same containing type).
- **v1.19 Workstream B** — Mutating-method lift admission. Expected effect: large recall-positive shift (lifts admit a substantial new candidate class — the entire `mutating func` surface that pre-v1.19 was vetoed at template entry); precision controlled by strict value-semantic admission gate + per-template scoring stack.

The cumulative effect direction is **net recall-positive** (Workstream C + Workstream B both introduce new candidates), with precision controlled by the existing scoring stack + the new carrier-kind signal. The cycle-14 numerator was 16 accepts; cycle-17 numerator is expected to grow if Workstreams B + C introduce new accepts, but the denominator is also expected to grow because the lifted surface is large.

The cycle-14 baseline rates were:

- monotonicity 5/6 = 83% (5 unknown)
- round-trip 9/19 = 47%
- associativity 1/5 = 20%
- commutativity 1/5 = 20%
- idempotence 0/12 = 0% — last measured 0%, still flat post-v1.16
- inverse-pair 0/1 = 0% — lone Algo stride-style pick, cycle-15 priority target
- identity-element 0/1 = 0% — `rescaledDivide(_:_:)` × `Complex.zero` outlier
- **Aggregate: 16/46 = 34.8%** (4 unknown excluded)

After cycles 15 + 16:

- **idempotence + round-trip + inverse-pair + identity-element** all gained the carrier-kind signal (Workstream A) → expected precision-positive shift on value-semantic struct carriers (no behavior change on reference-type carriers since they were already low-acceptance).
- **NEW dual-style consistency** template introduced (Workstream C) — adds a new accept class with no cycle-14 baseline; cycle-17 measures the first per-template rate.
- **NEW lifted-suggestion class** introduced (Workstream B) — four sub-templates (idempotence-lifted, composition, identity-element-lifted, inverse-pair-lifted) with no cycle-14 baseline; cycle-17 measures the first per-template rates.
- The cycle-14 0/12 idempotence rate is still **stale** (no new mechanisms target the non-lifted idempotence pool; carry-forward priorities #2 and #3 from cycle-16 specifically address this). The lifted idempotence sub-class is a fresh measurement.

**Cycle 17 measures whether the v1.18 + v1.19 mechanism work continued the cycle-14 trend (+8.1pp) or saturated/plateaued.** Three measurement points let the loop fit a trajectory rather than just compare two endpoints.

## What v1.20 ships

Five deliverables (mirroring v1.17's empirical-only cut):

1. **Surface re-capture (V1.20.A).** Re-run `swift-infer discover` against the four cycle-1..14 corpora at the v1.19 working tree. Capture the post-v1.19 candidate count per corpus + per template (including the new dual-style + lifted sub-classes). New `docs/calibration-cycle-17-data/surface-counts.md` documents the count per corpus per template, with delta tables vs cycle-6 (349) and cycle-14 (229).

2. **Triage rubric refresh (V1.20.B).** New `docs/cycle-17-triage-rubric.md` carries cycle-14's rubric verbatim and adds a "Post-cycle-14 mechanism context" section documenting the suppression layers a candidate has passed (cycles 15 + 16). New per-template criteria sections for the **new template classes** without cycle-14 baselines:
   - **Dual-style consistency** — accept criterion: the curated pair name describes a real dual-style sibling (mutating method's effect equals non-mutating sibling's return); reject criterion: name match without semantic correspondence (e.g., `add` / `adding` reused for non-paired operations).
   - **Lifted-suggestion class (4 sub-templates)** — accept criterion: the underlying `mutating func` has the algebraic property the lift asserts (e.g., a lifted `Counter.increment(by:)` accepts `CompositionTemplate` iff increment is genuinely additive); reject criterion: the lift admission was structurally sound but the underlying method violates the asserted law (e.g., `increment` clamps at a maximum or applies a non-linear transform).

3. **50-decision stratified sample on the v1.19 surface (V1.20.C).** New `docs/calibration-cycle-17-data/` with:

   - `triage-decisions.json` — machine-readable per-decision verdict (mirrors cycles 6 + 14).
   - `triage-notes.md` — per-decision human-readable rationale + verdict.

4. **Cycle-17 findings writeup (V1.20.D).** New `docs/calibration-cycle-17-findings.md` documenting:
   - Headline aggregate acceptance rate at v1.19 vs cycles 6 (26.7%) + 14 (34.8%).

   - Per-corpus rate breakdown vs cycle-14 baseline.
   - **Per-mechanism effectiveness ranking** — Workstream A precision contribution, Workstream C new-pick rate, Workstream B lifted-suggestion accept rate (broken down by sub-template).
   - Cycle-14 picks status: which picks were suppressed by which subsequent mechanism (continuation of the cycle-14 → cycle-17 closure).
   - Three-point trajectory analysis (cycles 6 / 14 / 17) — first cycle to support more than a two-point trend line.
   - Cycle-18 priority list rotated post-v1.20.

5. **Perf baseline (V1.20.E).** `docs/perf-baseline-v1.20.md` is a v1.19 carry-forward (zero behavior change → no re-measurement required, byte-stable suggestion stream). Mirrors v1.17's carry-forward posture.

6. **Version + CHANGELOG + README (V1.20.F).** CLI version `1.19.0` → `1.20.0`; CHANGELOG `[1.20.0]` entry covering the empirical-only release + the per-template rate-shift findings + the cycle-18 priority list; README status block + version pin + cycle-17 findings link refresh.

**Per-corpus weight expectations (provisional, refined at V1.20.A):**

## Open decisions

1. **Sample size: 50 (matches cycles 6 + 14) vs larger.** Default proposal: **(a) 50.** Rationale: direct comparability with cycles 6 + 14's per-template rates; same single-session rater fatigue level; the loop's stratification methodology is calibrated for 50. Larger sample (75 / 100) would buy statistical power at the cost of session fatigue + delayed v1.20 ship.

2. **Single-runner Claude vs multi-rater.** Default proposal: **(a) single-runner Claude** (matches cycles 6 + 14). Single-runner caveat documented in the rubric (carried forward verbatim).

3. **Stratification: cycle-14-matching weights vs proportional-to-surface vs new-class-weighted.** Default proposal: **(c) new-class-weighted** (per the table above — 35 existing + 15 new). Rationale: enables both per-template rate-shift on existing classes (≥ 8 picks each on round-trip / idempotence / monotonicity) and first per-template rates on new classes (3–5 picks each). Pure cycle-14-matching would leave the new classes unmeasured; pure proportional-to-surface would over-weight whichever class the lift admission produced the most candidates for and starve every other template.

4. **Cycle-14 picks reuse vs fresh sampling.** Default proposal: **(a) fresh stratified sampling** (matches cycles 6 + 14 posture). Rationale: preserves independent-measurement integrity; the cycle-14 picks are mostly suppressed (Workstream A's value-semantic carrier signal shifts several Likely → Strong, removing them from the Possible-tier surface). Fresh sampling produces a representative v1.19 view.

5. **Refresh rubric vs carry-forward verbatim.** Default proposal: **(b) carry-forward verbatim with cycle-17 supplement.** New `docs/cycle-17-triage-rubric.md` carries cycle-14's per-template criteria verbatim, adds a "Post-cycle-14 mechanism context" section documenting the suppression layers a candidate has passed (cycles 15 + 16), and adds new sections for the dual-style + 4 lifted sub-templates. The cycle-14 rubric stays unchanged.

6. **Tier mix: Possible-only (default) vs include some Likely-tier.** Default proposal: **(a) include cycle-14 carry-forwards** (the lone identity-element + lone inverse-pair Likely-tier outliers). Same as cycles 6 + 14 posture: outliers are included for cross-template coverage at fixed sample size.

7. **Lifted-suggestion class: 10 picks across 4 sub-templates (per provisional table) vs 5 picks across 4 sub-templates vs 15 picks.** Default proposal: **(a) 10 picks (3 + 3 + 2 + 2).** Rationale: each sub-template gets at least 2 picks so a first per-template rate can be reported (single-pick is too noisy); 10 total leaves enough budget for the 5 dual-style picks + 35 existing-class picks. Increasing to 15 would tighten lifted CIs at the cost of starving existing-class re-measurement.

8. **Cycle numbering: cycle 17 = v1.20.** Contiguous with v1.19 = cycle 16, v1.18 = cycle 15, v1.17 = cycle 14 (empirical), v1.16 = cycle 13. **Third empirical-only cycle** in the loop's history (after cycles 6 + 14).

9. **Whether to commit per-decision evidence files.** Default proposal: **yes** — the `triage-notes.md` per-decision rationale lets a future re-run validate Claude's verdicts; the `triage-decisions.json` machine-readable schema enables programmatic comparison with cycles 6 + 14.

10. **Whether V1.20.A's surface counts should also include the cycle-1..14 cumulative trend table.** Default proposal: **yes** — the surface-counts doc extends the cycle-14 findings §"Cumulative noise-floor trajectory" table by one more row (1167 → 229 → cycle-17 surface count) so the trajectory is captured in one place.

## v1.20 trajectory framing

PRD v1.0 §17.3 names calibration as "the operationalization of high precision, low recall."

- **Cycle 6 (v1.9)** introduced empirical measurement on the 349-surface — **26.7%** aggregate acceptance.
- **Cycle 14 (v1.17)** re-measured on the 229-surface — **34.8%** (+8.1pp).
- **Cycle 17 (v1.20, this release)** re-measures on the post-v1.19 surface — the loop's first **three-point trajectory** since cycle 6.

The measurement closes a meta-question that's been open since cycle 14:

> **Did the v1.18 + v1.19 mechanism work continue the cycle-14 trend (+8.1pp), saturate, or overshoot? And how do the new template classes (dual-style + lifted) compare to the existing-class re-measurement?**

Cycle 17's headline number answers this directly. Possible outcomes (for narrative-doc planning):

- **Outcome A: Aggregate ≥ 50%.** Suppression + new-class introduction is paying off; the loop is on trajectory toward §19's ≥70% target. Continue mechanism cycles toward the carry-forward priorities.
- **Outcome B: Aggregate 38–50%.** Continued improvement; the v1.18 + v1.19 mechanisms shifted the rate further in the cycle-14 direction. New-class rates likely high (per-construction precision); existing-class rates likely flat-to-up.
- **Outcome C: Aggregate ≈ 34.8% (flat vs cycle 14).** v1.18 + v1.19 contributed precision-only — new accepts (Workstream B + C) match new rejects. Saturation signal — pivot to v1.1+ trajectory (SemanticIndex, IDE integration).
- **Outcome D: Aggregate < 34.8%.** Counter-intuitive; either the lifted surface is high-noise (Workstream B over-shipped) or the carrier-kind signal under-suppressed reference-type carriers. Methodology problem — investigate per-mechanism effectiveness ranking to identify which workstream overshot. May trigger a v1.21 revert-cycle or a magnitude re-baselining (per cycle-16 carry-forward priority #8).

Whichever outcome lands, the cycle-18 priority list rotates accordingly:

- **Outcome A or B**: continue mechanism cycles (cycle-16 carry-forwards: math-library forward-function counter, fixed-point-name positive signal, FP approximate-equality, etc.).
- **Outcome C**: explicit pivot to PRD §20 v1.1+ items (SemanticIndex first).
- **Outcome D**: revert-cycle to identify which mechanism overshot; may downgrade `liftedFromMutation` magnitude from +10 to +5 or relax `CompositionTemplate` numeric-only gate.

After v1.20 ships:

- The loop has measured aggregate rates **three times** (cycles 6 + 14 + 17) and shipped **three new mechanism cycles** between cycles 14 and 17 (cycles 15 + 16; cycle 14 itself was empirical). Cadence: every 3 mechanism cycles → 1 empirical cycle.
- The next empirical cycle would naturally land at cycle 20 (every 3 mechanism cycles), unless cycle-17 outcome demands an earlier re-measurement.

The natural cadence stays at one mechanism per release. After cycle 17's measurement data, the §19 acceptance-rate trajectory should be projectable from three points: if the trajectory is clearly headed toward 70%, continue mechanism cycles; if it's plateaued, pivot. v1.20 is the data point that lets that judgment land empirically.

No active milestone plan opens with the v1.20 commit. Subsequent work picks one of the §20 items, the post-v1.20 priority list, or a fresh refactor target and opens a fresh `docs/<plan>.md`.
