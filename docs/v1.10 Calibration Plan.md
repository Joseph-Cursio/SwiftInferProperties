# v1.10 Calibration Plan

Working doc for the SwiftInferProperties v1.10 cut. v1.10 is the **seventh calibration cycle** — closes cycle-7 priority #1 from `docs/calibration-cycle-6-findings.md` (idempotence template counter-signal on direction-named labels) and runs the §17.3 loop a seventh time. **First data-driven cycle**: v1.10's structural change is anchored on cycle-6's measured 0/10 (0%) idempotence acceptance rate, not on conjecture.

> **v1.10 is the first cycle whose mechanism is empirically motivated.** Cycles 1-5 each shipped one structural mechanism with conjectural justification; cycle 6 measured the resulting Possible-tier acceptance rate; cycle 7 picks the strongest measured signal (idempotence at 0/10) and applies a targeted counter-signal. The empirical loop now closes through one full revolution: hypothesis → mechanism → measurement → tuning.

**Ephemeral** — archive to `docs/archive/v1.10 Calibration Plan.md` once the tag is pushed and the §13 + §16 release-blocking criteria still hold.

## Context: cycle-6 surfaced the strongest measurement signal

Per `docs/calibration-cycle-6-findings.md`: idempotence's 0/10 Possible-tier acceptance rate was the cycle's strongest scoring-tuning signal. All 10 sampled idempotence rejections were `(T) -> T` *directional* operations:

```
Sampled idempotence rejections (cycle-6 picks #17-#27):
  index(after:)    [×3 sites]  (Index) -> Index
  index(before:)   [×1 site]   (Index) -> Index
  bucket(after:)               (Bucket) -> Bucket
  endOfChunk(startingAt:)      (Base.Index) -> Base.Index
  minimumCapacity(forScale:)   (Int) -> Int      [domain-mismatch, separate cause]
  _minimumCapacity(forScale:)  (Int) -> Int      [domain-mismatch, separate cause]
```

Two sub-patterns:
- **Direction-labeled (T) -> T** (5 of 10 rejects): functions whose argument label is `after`, `before`, `next`, `prev`, etc. Incrementing twice ≠ incrementing once — idempotence is structurally false for these.
- **Domain-mismatch (T) -> T** (3 of 10 rejects): scale-vs-capacity, where input and output share `Int` *type* but represent semantically distinct domains. Idempotence fails because the function's output is in a different sub-domain than its input.

V1.10.1 closes the direction-labeled pattern. The domain-mismatch pattern is a separate cycle-8 candidate (semantic intent isn't textually inferrable from labels alone — would need parameter-name vs return-name semantic matching).

## What v1.10 ships

Two deliverables:

1. **`IdempotenceTemplate` direction-label counter-signal.** New `Signal.Kind.directionLabel` (-15 weight, non-veto) emitted by `IdempotenceTemplate.suggest(...)` when the candidate's first parameter has an argument label matching a curated direction set:
   ```swift
   directionLabels: Set<String> = [
       "after", "before",
       "next", "prev", "previous",
       "advance", "succ", "pred", "successor", "predecessor"
   ]
   ```
   Score arithmetic:
   - typeSymmetry alone: +30 → Possible tier (20-39 range).
   - typeSymmetry + direction counter: +30 - 15 = +15 → Suppressed tier (< 20).
   - typeSymmetry + curated verb match (`normalize`, `canonicalize`, etc.) + direction counter: +30 + 40 - 15 = +55 → Likely tier (still surfaced).
   
   Well-named idempotent operations that happen to have a direction label keep surfacing; direction-only-style ops drop below the Possible threshold.

2. **Cycle-7 calibration run + findings writeup.** Re-run `swift-infer discover --include-possible` against the four cycle-1+2+3+4+5+6 corpora at the v1.10 commit; capture post-counter-signal snapshots to `docs/calibration-cycle-7-data/`. Compare suppression deltas vs the cycle-5 post-tightening baseline (the 349-surface; cycle-6 didn't move it). Findings doc at `docs/calibration-cycle-7-findings.md` documents per-corpus delta + verification (re-test the cycle-6 idempotence picks against the v1.10 binary; expect 5 of 10 to suppress) + cycle-8 hypotheses.

PRD §19 sets the long-term acceptance-rate target at ≥ 70% after 6 months of dogfooding; v1.10 is cycle 7 of that loop and the first to *target* a measured rate-improvement.

- **Other six templates untouched.** Cycle-7 priority #2 (inverse-pair tightening) ships in v1.11; cycle-7 priority #5 (round-trip directional counter-signal) ships in v1.12. Bundling all three muddies attribution.

## Open decisions

1. **Counter-signal weight.** Two choices: (a) -15 (this plan); (b) -10 (matches `floatingPointStorage` posture). Default proposal: **(a) -15.** Rationale: -15 cleanly drops Possible (+30) into Suppressed (<20). -10 would leave the score at +20, which is the *boundary* between Possible and Suppressed — could land in either tier depending on Score-tier boundary inclusion rules. -15 has clean margin.

2. **Curated direction-label set.** Two choices: (a) narrow `{after, before, next, prev}` (the cycle-6-observed subset); (b) broader `{after, before, next, prev, previous, advance, succ, pred, successor, predecessor}` (this plan). Default proposal: **(b) broader.** Rationale: covers Swift stdlib's `Strideable.advance(by:)`, `BidirectionalCollection.index(before:)`, `IteratorProtocol.next()` analogues, and Comparable's `predecessor()`-like patterns. The broader set's marginal cost is tiny (10-element string Set), and false-positive risk is low — these labels are nearly always direction-style.

3. **Where the counter-signal helper lives.** Two choices: (a) private static func on `IdempotenceTemplate` (this plan); (b) shared utility on `FunctionSummary`. Default proposal: **(a) private helper.** Rationale: only `IdempotenceTemplate.suggest(...)` consumes it. Cycle-7 priorities #2 + #5 will create similar helpers on `InversePairTemplate` and `RoundTripTemplate`; sharing across three templates is a v1.13+ refactor concern.

4. **Whether to commit cycle-7 decisions data.** Default: **yes** — `docs/calibration-cycle-7-data/` mirroring cycles 1-5. Files small.

5. **Whether to bundle cycle-7 priorities #2 + #5 into v1.10.** Two choices: (a) ship priority #1 only (this plan); (b) bundle 1+2+5 (all direction-label counter-signals). Default proposal: **(a) priority #1 only.** Rationale: same "one rule per release" precedent as v1.5-1.9. Bundling muddies per-template attribution.

6. **Re-run cadence.** Default: **post-counter-signal only** — cycle-5's post-tightening snapshots are the cycle-7 pre-counter-signal baseline (cycle-6 didn't move the surface).

7. **Tier-boundary collision risk.** The cycle-6 idempotence picks include items at Score 30 + Score 25 + others. The counter-signal -15 against +30 baseline: 15 → Suppressed. Against +25 baseline: 10 → Suppressed. Against +50 (curated verb): 35 → Possible. Default: this is the desired behavior — high-confidence verbs override the structural penalty.

## v1.10 trajectory framing

PRD v1.0 §17.3 names calibration as "the operationalization of high precision, low recall — without it, the philosophy is just a slogan." Cycle 7 is the first cycle to ship a structural change *targeted at a measured signal* rather than a conjectured one. After v1.10 ships:

- The cycle-6 0/10 idempotence acceptance rate has a measurement-anchored fix in-tree.
- Cycle-8 can re-measure (sample anew, or re-triage the cycle-6 picks at the v1.10 binary) and confirm whether the rate moves up.
- The data-driven priority list scales: cycles 8 + 9 + 10 will pick up priorities #2 + #3 + #4 + #5 from cycle-6's findings.

The natural cadence stays at one cycle per release. After cycle 10 or so, the §19 acceptance-rate target should be measurable across multiple per-template improvements.

After the calibration loop reaches steady state, the natural next contributions return to the §20 v1.1+ trajectory.

No active milestone plan opens with the v1.10 commit. Subsequent work picks one of the §20 items and opens a fresh `docs/<plan>.md`.
