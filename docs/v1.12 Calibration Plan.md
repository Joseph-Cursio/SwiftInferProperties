## What v1.12 ships

Two deliverables (mirroring the v1.10 + v1.11 shape):

1. **`RoundTripTemplate` direction-label counter-signal.** Re-uses the existing `Signal.Kind.directionLabel` enum case shipped in v1.10 (now consumed by three templates). New private helper `RoundTripTemplate.directionLabelCounterSignal(for:)` returning weight `-15` (note **−15**, mirroring v1.10 idempotence — see open decision #1) when *either* side of the pair's first-parameter argument label matches the curated direction set:
   ```swift
   // Reuses IdempotenceTemplate.directionLabels — see open decision #2.
   ```
   Score arithmetic for round-trip (baseline `+30` typeSymmetry, matching idempotence):
   - typeSymmetry alone: `+30` → Possible tier (20-39 range, lower end).
   - typeSymmetry + direction counter: `+30 − 15 = +15` → Suppressed tier (< 20).
   - typeSymmetry + curated/project name (`+40`) + direction counter: `+30 + 40 − 15 = +55` → Likely tier (clean preservation, well above the 40 boundary).
   - typeSymmetry + `@Discoverable(group:)` (`+35`) + direction counter: `+30 + 35 − 15 = +50` → Likely tier (preserved).
   - typeSymmetry + cross-type counter (`-25`) + direction counter: `+30 − 25 − 15 = -10` → Suppressed tier (deeper margin; both counters compose correctly when the pair is both cross-type AND direction-labeled).

   Curated round-trip name matches (`encode/decode`, `parse/format`, `compress/decompress`, …) keep surfacing comfortably even if a direction label coincidentally appears; bare-shape directional pairs drop below the Possible threshold.

2. **Cycle-9 calibration run + findings writeup.** Re-run `swift-infer discover --include-possible` against the four cycle-1+2+3+4+5+6+7+8 corpora at the v1.12 commit; capture post-counter-signal snapshots to `docs/calibration-cycle-9-data/`. Compare suppression deltas vs the cycle-8 post-inverse-direction-counter baseline (the 288-surface). Findings doc at `docs/calibration-cycle-9-findings.md` documents per-corpus delta + the **direction-counter-family completion** framing ("first cycle to land the same mechanism on a third template, demonstrating the curated direction-label set is template-agnostic and the `Signal.Kind.directionLabel` factoring scales") + cycle-10 hypotheses.

PRD §19 sets the long-term acceptance-rate target at ≥ 70% after 6 months of dogfooding; v1.12 is cycle 9 of that loop.

## Open decisions

1. **Counter-signal weight: -15 vs -10.** Two choices: (a) **-15 (this plan's default; mirrors v1.10 idempotence verbatim)**; (b) -10 (mirrors v1.11 inverse-pair). Default proposal: **(a) -15.** Rationale: round-trip's typeSymmetry baseline is `+30`, matching idempotence's `+30` (not inverse-pair's `+25`). The math:
   - **-15**: bare → `+15` Suppressed (clean margin from 20 boundary); curated name (`+40`) + direction counter → `+55` Likely (clean preservation, well above the 40 boundary); discoverable (`+35`) + direction counter → `+50` Likely (preserved).
   - **-10**: bare → `+20` Possible (sits *exactly on* the tier boundary — the noisy zone v1.10 / v1.11's open-decision-#1 explicitly avoided).

   The -15 weight matches v1.10's calibration verbatim because the score baselines match. v1.11's -10 was specifically calibrated for inverse-pair's lower +25 baseline. Conservative-precision posture (PRD §3.5) prefers the cleaner-margin option, and the v1.10 → v1.12 ports the *exact same arithmetic* on templates with the *exact same baseline*.

2. **Where the curated `directionLabels` set lives — third consumer.** Three choices: (a) **continue reusing `IdempotenceTemplate.directionLabels` directly (this plan)**; (b) hoist to `SwiftInferCore.DirectionLabels` *as part of v1.12*; (c) duplicate as `RoundTripTemplate.directionLabels`. Default proposal: **(a) continue reuse.** Rationale: the v1.11 open-decision-#2 commitment was "hoist as a v1.13 atomic move when round-trip becomes the third consumer." That commitment stands — *bundling* the hoist with v1.12 would muddy attribution between (i) the structural change (counter-signal) and (ii) the refactor (hoist). Same pattern as v1.6.1 (math-library op gate) shipping separately from v1.7's structural bake-in. Continued reuse makes v1.12 a clean one-rule release; v1.13 ships the hoist as a clean zero-behavior-change refactor.

3. **Either-side vs forward-only detection.** Two choices: (a) **either-side (this plan)** — fires when forward's *or* reverse's first-param label is in the curated set; (b) forward-only. Default proposal: **(a) either-side.** Rationale: the dominant Algo pattern is `index(after:) × index(before:)` — both sides direction-labeled, but a forward-only check would still fire because forward has `after`. The interesting edge case is *asymmetric* labeling: `transform(_:) × untransform(after:)` — forward has no label, reverse has `after`. Forward-only would miss this; either-side catches it. False-positive risk: a curated-name pair with one direction-labeled side (e.g., `encode(_:) × decode(after:)`) — but the curated name's `+40` keeps it in Likely tier with -15 counter (`+30 + 40 − 15 = +55`). Either-side detection is strictly more sensitive without false-positive cost given the -15 weight.

4. **Whether to commit cycle-9 decisions data.** Default: **yes** — `docs/calibration-cycle-9-data/` mirroring cycles 1-8. Files small.

5. **Whether to bundle cycle-9 priorities #1 + #2 (SetAlgebra-shape detection on inverse-pair) into v1.12.** Two choices: (a) **ship priority #1 only (this plan)**; (b) bundle 1+2. Default proposal: **(a) priority #1 only.** Rationale: same "one rule per release" precedent as v1.5-1.11. Bundling muddies per-template attribution. Different signal class too — direction-label counter is text-of-label-based; SetAlgebra-shape is protocol-coverage-based.

6. **Re-run cadence.** Default: **post-counter-signal only** — cycle-8's post-inverse-direction-counter snapshots at `docs/calibration-cycle-8-data/` are the cycle-9 pre-counter-signal baseline. No need to re-run cycle-8's snapshots.

7. **Pre-existing round-trip signals' interaction with the direction counter.** The template currently emits six signals: `typeSymmetrySignature` (+30), `exactNameMatch` (+40, curated/project), `discoverableAnnotation` (+35), `crossTypeRoundTripPair` (-25, V1.4.3b counter), `nonDeterministicBody` (veto), `protocolCoverageVeto` (V1.5.2 + V1.8.1 shape-gated veto). The new direction counter is the second non-veto negative signal alongside `crossType`. Score interaction matrix (most relevant cells):
   ```
                           Direction:           no       yes (-15)
   crossType: no    Bare:                 +30 P    +15 SUPP
                    Curated name (+40):   +70 LIK  +55 LIK
                    Discoverable (+35):   +65 LIK  +50 LIK
   crossType: yes (-25)  Bare:            +5  SUPP -10 SUPP
                         Curated name:    +45 LIK  +30 P
                         Discoverable:    +40 LIK  +25 P
   ```
   All three counter-signal combinations (cross-type alone, direction alone, both) compose into the right tier. The "cross-type + curated name + direction" triple at `+30 P` is interesting: the user explicitly named the curated pair (`encode/decode`) AND the structural counters both fire — Possible is the right tier (preserve for review with an explanation). Default: this is the desired behavior, no veto-collapse needed.

8. **Tier-boundary collision risk.** Cycle-8 round-trip Possible-tier suggestions all surfaced at Score 30 (Possible). The counter -15 against +30 baseline: 15 → Suppressed (clean margin from 20). Against +70 (curated): 55 → Likely (clean margin from 75 Strong). Against +65 (discoverable): 50 → Likely (clean). Default: this matches the design intent — direction-labeled bare-shape pairs suppress; curated/project name matches preserve at Likely.

9. **File-split: pre-emptive vs reactive.** Two choices: (a) **pre-emptive file-split (this plan)** — extract `directionLabelCounterSignal(for:)` to `RoundTripDirectionLabelCounter.swift` upfront; (b) reactive — inline first, split if swiftlint trips. Default proposal: **(a) pre-emptive.** Rationale: `RoundTripTemplate.swift` is already at 348 lines (332-line enum body), well over swiftlint's default `type_body_length: 250` warning threshold and within 17 lines of the 350 hard error. Adding ~20 lines for the counter helper would breach. The V1.6.1 / V1.8.1 / V1.10.1 / V1.11.1 file-split precedent applies here for the same reason it applied to inverse-pair (which was 1 line over budget). Pre-emptive split avoids a "first commit fails CI, second commit fixes" round-trip during V1.12.1.

## v1.12 trajectory framing

PRD v1.0 §17.3 names calibration as "the operationalization of high precision, low recall — without it, the philosophy is just a slogan."

Cycle 7 (v1.10) was the first cycle to ship a structural change *targeted at a measured signal*.
Cycle 8 (v1.11) was the first to *replicate a verified mechanism on an adjacent template* — confirming v1.10's `Signal.Kind.directionLabel` enum case + `IdempotenceTemplate.directionLabels` set work cross-template at calibrated weights.
Cycle 9 (v1.12) is the first to *complete the three-template direction-counter family* — confirming the abstraction scales beyond two consumers and giving v1.13 the natural moment to hoist to a shared namespace as a zero-behavior-change refactor.

The pattern confirms a **mechanism-development cadence**: introduce in cycle N, replicate in cycle N+1, complete the family in cycle N+2, hoist the abstraction in cycle N+3. v1.10 → v1.11 → v1.12 → v1.13 is the first instance of this cadence playing out. Future mechanisms (domain-mismatch detection, FP approximate-equality, SetAlgebra-shape coverage candidates) will likely follow the same shape.

After v1.12 ships:
- The cycle-6 0/14 round-trip rejected-picks (re-triaged at the v1.12 binary) should produce a measurable acceptance-rate shift on the round-trip template specifically.
- The data-driven priority list scales: cycles 10 + 11 + 12 will pick up v1.13 hoist + SetAlgebra-shape detection + domain-mismatch family + stride-style label extension.
- The aggregate suppression count will likely cross the **75% cumulative reduction milestone** (1167 → 257 = 78.0%).

The natural cadence stays at one cycle per release. After cycle 11 or so, the §19 acceptance-rate target should be measurable across multiple per-template improvements, and the calibration loop's saturation should become visible (when re-sampling the cycle-1 picks at v1.13+ produces ≥ 70% acceptance, the loop has reached its target).

After the calibration loop reaches steady state, the natural next contributions return to the §20 v1.1+ trajectory.

No active milestone plan opens with the v1.12 commit. Subsequent work picks one of the §20 items, the v1.13 hoist commitment, or the cycle-10+ priority list and opens a fresh `docs/<plan>.md`.
